FIRST-AUTHOR RESEARCH · 2025—2026

↗

AgileDP

Quantizing Diffusion Policy via Dynamic Scaling and Selective Distillation

CoRL submission · First author

Diffusion Policy achieves strong manipulation performance through iterative denoising, but its inference cost makes edge deployment difficult. I analyzed timestep-dependent activation shifts and the uneven importance of information across action trajectories, then designed a low-bit quantization method combining dynamic scaling over time with selective knowledge distillation.

REAL-WORLD ROBOTICS · 2025—2026

Real-world Imitation Learning

ACT & Diffusion Policy on Franka Panda

I adapted ACT, originally used primarily with ALOHA, for the Franka Panda by extending the teleoperation, data collection, training, inference, and control stack. I replaced Temporal Ensemble with polynomial-interpolation control to reduce inference overhead and improve trajectory continuity.

Within the same experimental workspace, I implemented U-Net- and DiT-based Diffusion Policies and compared real-robot performance on long-horizon tasks such as door opening and pick-and-place. I also built a MuJoCo-based Sim2Real workspace for reproducible evaluation.

REINFORCEMENT LEARNING · 2024—2025

Mobile Manipulation RL

Single PPO → Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning

I improved a single PPO policy for mobile-manipulator door opening by revising the reward function, collision conditions, gripper control, and termination logic. The environment was debugged step by step with attention to kinematics, dynamics, and contact behavior.

After identifying the limits of a single policy on multi-stage, long-horizon tasks, I extended the system into a hierarchical policy with a high-level task generator and low-level controller. I reproduced the iGibson results and converted the environment to MuJoCo to evaluate reproducibility across simulators.

  • PPO
  • Hierarchical RL
  • Mobile Manipulator
  • Kinematics
  • Dynamics

SIMULATION · 2026

Scalable Neural Simulator

Articulated rigid-body simulation and Sim2Real evaluation

I led technical ablations for a neural simulator designed for articulated rigid bodies, analyzing physical simulation components including collision handling, numerical integration, and state transitions.

I built an evaluation pipeline that compares Isaac, RaiSim, MJX, MuJoCo-CPU, and the in-house simulator under consistent conditions. Rather than using downstream RL performance as an indirect proxy, I proposed a Sim2Real metric that directly measures agreement between simulation and real-world trajectories; the metric was adopted by the project.

MODEL QUANTIZATION · 2024

Report ↗

PTMQ+

Multi-bit Weight & Activation Post-Training Quantization

I reimplemented the PTMQ paper in PyTorch despite the absence of released source code, reproducing block reconstruction, the Multi-bit Feature Mixer, and Group-Distill Loss, and published the implementation as open source.

I proposed PTMQ-W, extending activation-focused multi-bit PTQ to weights, and developed PTMQ+, which supports variable precision for both weights and activations. The method was evaluated on ResNet, MobileNet, RegNet, and ViT using ImageNet.

EDGE ROBOT VISION · 2026

Delivery Robot Invoice VLM

Offline parcel-address recognition on Jetson Orin NX

I developed an end-to-end pipeline that enables a delivery robot to recognize parcel labels from camera images and extract building, unit, and recipient information. YOLO OBB detects and crops the label, while a LoRA-tuned InternVL3-2B generates structured fields.

I implemented distance-aware autofocus using ROS2 and a depth camera and deployed the system on JetPack 6.1 and 7.2. Because no official 7.2 image was available, I built TensorRT-LLM from source and applied SmoothQuant W8A8, meeting the offline constraints of sub-0.5-second end-to-end latency and more than 98% field accuracy.
